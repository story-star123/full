# The Red Window

Blackwood Manor sat at the end of a forgotten road, shrouded in perpetual fog. Locals whispered about the family that once lived there, about the strange experiments, and the night they all vanished.

Sarah, an avid urban explorer, didn't believe in ghosts. She adjusted her camera strap and pushed open the heavy oak doors. The interior was a frozen tableau of the past, thick with dust and the smell of decay.

As she made her way to the second floor, a strange chill settled in her bones. The temperature dropped significantly. She stopped, her breath forming clouds in the air. Down the hall, a single door stood ajar, a sickly red light spilling out from within.

Against her better judgment, she moved towards it. The floorboards creaked in protest. She peered inside. The room was empty, save for a large mirror on the far wall. The red light seemed to emanate from the glass itself.

Sarah raised her camera, but as she looked through the viewfinder, her blood ran cold. In the reflection, she wasn't alone. Dozens of hollow-eyed figures stood behind her, their mouths open in silent screams. 

She lowered the camera. The room was empty. She raised it again. They were closer. And then, the red light began to flicker, and the whispering started.
