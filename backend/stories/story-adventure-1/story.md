# The Floating Ruins of Aethelgard

For months, the map had been his only companion. Silas had braved treacherous jungles, scaled sheer cliffs, and navigated rivers teeming with predators, all for a legend. They called it Aethelgard, the city in the sky.

As he crested the final peak of the Wyrmtooth Mountains, the breath caught in his throat. Before him, nestled in a hidden valley, giant chunks of earth and stone floated effortlessly in the air, defying gravity. Waterfalls cascaded from the floating islands, dissipating into mist before they hit the ground below.

Crumbling stone bridges connected some of the larger landmasses, remnants of a civilization long gone. Silas secured his grappling hook and swung across the abyss to the nearest island. 

The architecture was breathtaking—intricate carvings of mythical beasts adorned tall spires that reached for the clouds. As he explored deeper into the ruins, he found a central plaza. In its center, a massive crystal pulsed with a soft, azure light.

He approached it, his hand trembling. As his fingers brushed the smooth surface of the crystal, a flood of knowledge washed over him. The history, the magic, the reason for the city's ascent—it all became clear. Aethelgard hadn't been abandoned; it had been preserved. And Silas was its new guardian.
